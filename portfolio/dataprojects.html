Data Projects

Music Trends

For this project, I wanted to understand how music trends evolve and what really defines success in the industry. I analyzed datasets on best-selling artists, Billboard chart performance, and Grammy winners, then compared them with current Spotify data. What stood out most was how different the results look depending on the metric — sales don’t always match awards, and chart performance doesn’t always reflect streaming popularity. This project pushed me to think critically about data sources, bias, and how the story changes depending on what you choose to measure.

work flow of cu boulder student em

For this project, my team and I conducted interviews with members of the CU Boulder Student Ambassadors program and used Miro boards to perform thematic analysis. By identifying recurring patterns in workflows and information systems, we developed design principles and proposed actionable improvements to strengthen organizational processes. This project strengthened my skills in qualitative research, synthesis, and translating insights into thoughtful, practical design recommendations.

Exploring Distributed Cognition

For this project, I conducted an in-person observational study at Qdoba in Boulder to understand how a fast-casual restaurant functions as an information system. I analyzed how the spatial layout, menu placement, role coordination, and step-by-step ordering process guide customers through decision-making and food preparation. By mapping the space and tracing how information moves from menu to employee to customer, I developed design recommendations to improve clarity and labeling. This project deepened my understanding of how physical space, coordination, and visual cues shape user experience in everyday systems.
